    I grew up in a very large family where I didn't have to do any coooking. I was the 9th child of 10 and things were so crazy. I didn't know how to cook, in fact I was terrified of the kitchen.

    I was in high school and I had heard our cooking department was good and that some classes even offered college credit. I was intrigued but scared. I decided cooking was a useful skill and I needed to overcome my fears. So I took a beginning cooking class.

Eggs Benedict

A New York Original

  • Ingredients:
    • Eggs
    • My Easy Homemade Hollandaise Sauce
    • Bacon (whichever type of bacon you prefer)
    • Toast or English Muffin
  • Directions:
    1. Cook bacon (roughly 15 minutes)
    2. Add salt and vinegar to a pot of water. Heat to almost boiling, about 190 degrees F
    3. Create a Slight whirlpool with a spoon in the pot and add whole egg from small bowl. Cook egg for 2-3 minutes, depending on how you like the yolk.
    4. Serve on toast. Layer as toast, bacon, egg, and hollandaise sauce on top. GArnish with either chives or dill and enjoy!

Beef Wellington

British Classic

  • Ingredients:
    • Center-Cut Beef Tenderloin, roughly 2 lbs. (Chateaubriand)
    • Mushrooms
    • Salt and Pepper
    • Olive Oil
    • Dijon Mustard
    • The leaves of 1 sprig of Thyme
    • 1 Shallot
    • 2 tbsp. Unsalted Butter
    • 12 slices of Proscuitto
    • all-purpose flour, for dusting and rolling
    • Puff Pastry
    • 1 Beaten Egg
    • Flaky Salt for garnishing
  • Directions:
    1. Tie tenderloin in several places to ensure uniform shape. Season generously with salt and freshly cracked black pepper.
    2. Heat heavy bottom skillet over high heat. Coat pan with olive oil. Once pan is nearly smoking, add tenderloin and sear til nice browning occurs on all sides. About 2 minutes per side. Transfer to plate and let cool. When cooled, remove twine and coat all sides with mustard.
    3. While tenderloin is browning, make the mushroom duxelle. Either by hand or in a food processor, finely chop mushrooms, shallot, and thyme.
    4. Add butter to pan and melt over medium heat. Add mushrooms mixture and cook until liquid has completely evaporated from pan, about 20-25 minutes. Season wit salt and pepper and cool mixture.
    5. Place a good amount of plastice wrap on your work surface, overlapping so that it's 2x the size of the tenderloin in all directions. Overlap the prosciutto on the plastice wrap into a rectangle that is big enough to cover the entire tenderloin. Spread mushroom duxelle evenly over the prosciutto.
    6. Season tenderloin and place at the bottom of the prosciutto. Roll the prosciutto and mushrooms around the tenderloin and use plastice wrap to help get it wrapped tightly. Try to get a uniform shape and twist ends of plastic wrap tightly into a log and chill in fridge (chilling helps maintain the shape of the log)
    7. Pre-heat oven to 425 degrees F. Lightly flour worksurface and spread into rectangle that will cover your tenderloin. remove tnederlion from plastic and place on bottom of puff pastry. brush edges with egg wash, then roll tightly into a nice log shape. If you want a more consistent shape, add into more plastic wrap and roll tightly and twist ends of plastic wrap to develop shape and place in fridge for 20-30 minutes.
    8. Once the wellington is ready to be cooking, trim excess pastry and crimp edges. You can use tools to make the wellington look fancier, I use a lattice roller on more pastry to create the pattern on top but designs can be marked into the top with a small knife as well.
    9. Transfer Wellington to a foil-lined baking sheet. Brush with egg wash, and garnish with flaky salt.
    10. Bake until pastry is golden and the beef registers around 120 degrees F for a medium rare wellington, 40-45 minutes. Let it rest for a good 10 minutes before carving, and then enjoy your hard work!

Indian Curry

A Flavor Packed Dream

  • Ingredients:
    • Chicken Breast (about 2 lbs)
    • Plain Whole Milk Yoghurt
    • Lemon Juice
    • Spices:
      • Curry Powder
      • Garam Masala
      • Turmeric
      • Salt
      • Black Pepper
      • Cumin
      • Fennel
      • Fenugreek
      • Cayenne
      • Cinnamon
      • Coriander
    • Ghee (Clarified Butter or just regular butter)
    • 1 Red Onion
    • Fresh Garlic Cloves(to taste)
    • Fresh Ginger
    • 4 Roma Tomatoes
    • 3 Serrano Peppers
    • 3 Cups Chicken Stock
    • 1 1/2 Cups Cream
    • Fresh Cilantro
  • Directions:
    1. Start preparing the chicken breast the night before. Cut chicken into into pieces just over an inch. Place into a bowl large enough to hold all the chicken. Add just enough lemon juice to coat the chicken as well as a little but of salt. Let this sit for 15 minutes. Combine about a cup of the yoghurt with the chicken and also mix in some curry powder and garam masala (if wanted, you can also add some ginger and garlic here as well). Cover and place on bottom shelf of the fridge overnight.
    2. Prepare onion, tomato, garlic, ginger, cilantro and serrano peppers. Finely dice onion, tomatoes, ginger, and garlic. Cut lower stems off cilantro, the rest will be chopped later.
    3. Melt Ghee/butter over medium-high heat in a large pot, preferably a cast-iron dutch oven. Once ghee/butter is melted and hot, add chicken in 2 batches, cooking until browned but not fully cooked. Don't worry, you will finish cooking through later. Remove Chicken from pan and set aside for later.
    4. Add more ghee/butter and then add onion and saute. Once slightly browned, add the ginger and garlic. Let cook for about 1 minute. Then add the tomato and cook for a minute. Then add all of your spices and let it combine for about a minute. At this point, add chicken stock. Cover and bring to a simmer and reduce heat to medium-low and let simmer for 5 minutes.
    5. Add the chicken back into sauce and cover and bring to a simmer for 10 minutes. The chicken should be cooked through at this point. Remove from the heat and add heavy cream. Stir to combine. Chop cilantro leaves. Serve curry with a cilantro garnish and enjoy!
